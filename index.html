The honest truth

Most productivity apps were built for people who don't have ADHD.

🌀

Paralysis by infinite lists

Opening a 40-item todo list shouldn't feel like staring into an abyss. But for ADHD minds, seeing everything at once triggers avoidance, not action.

⏳

Time blindness is real

It's not laziness. ADHD brains genuinely struggle to feel time passing. Hours vanish. Deadlines appear. The morning you planned falls apart by 9am.

🔁

The "fresh start" cycle

New app. New system. New hope. Then the guilt. Then the abandonment. Then another new app. FOCAL is built to survive contact with real ADHD life.

How FOCAL works

Calm.
Gentle.
One step.

01

Brain dump, then breathe

Throw everything in. Don't sort it. Don't prioritise. Just get it out of your head. FOCAL holds it safely without demanding order.

02

Pick just three things

FOCAL asks you: what are the three things that would make today feel meaningful? That's your whole day. Nothing else is shown.

03

Work on one at a time

The screen shows only your current task. Gentle timers. No notifications. No sidebar screaming at you. Just this, right now.

04

Close the day kindly

No guilt for unfinished tasks. FOCAL acknowledges what you did, rolls over what you didn't, and helps you finish on your terms.

Built different, for a reason

Every detail is designed to reduce friction for ADHD minds.

Core principle

One task on screen.
Always.

The single biggest cause of ADHD overwhelm is seeing too much at once. FOCAL's radical simplicity means your brain only ever has to deal with one thing. Not two. One.

Time awareness

Gentle time anchors

Soft ambient timers help you feel time without the pressure of a countdown. You'll know it's been 30 minutes. You won't panic about it.

Energy matching

How are you, actually?

Log your energy at the start of each day. FOCAL adapts — suggesting shorter tasks when you're running low, and protecting your peak hours for deep work.

No shame

Compassionate rollover

Didn't finish? FOCAL doesn't make you feel bad about it. Tasks roll over gently, with a note that it's okay. Tomorrow is a new chance.

Distraction guard

Capture and return

A thought pops into your head? Quick-capture it in one keystroke and return immediately to what you were doing. No rabbit holes.
